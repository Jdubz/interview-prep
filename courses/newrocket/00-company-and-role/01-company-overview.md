# 01 – newRocket Company Overview

Everything you need to know about newRocket before walking into an interview. This isn't trivia — understanding the company's business model, competitive position, and strategic direction lets you speak their language and frame your experience in terms that matter to them.

---

## 1. What newRocket Is

newRocket is the **largest pure-play ServiceNow Elite partner**. "Pure-play" is the key word — unlike Accenture, Deloitte, or Infosys, newRocket does nothing but ServiceNow. 100% of their revenue, talent, and strategy is focused on one platform.

**By the numbers:**
- ~$140M revenue
- 1,000+ enterprise clients globally
- 4,000+ completed projects
- 501–1,000 employees
- Offices: Vista CA (HQ), New York, London, Pune India
- One of only **two partners worldwide** with all 4 ServiceNow workflow badges (IT, Customer, Employee, Creator)

### How They Make Money

newRocket is a professional services firm. Revenue comes from:
1. **Advisory consulting** — helping enterprises plan their ServiceNow strategy
2. **Implementation services** — building and deploying ServiceNow solutions
3. **Managed services** — ongoing support and optimization
4. **AI products & accelerators** — pre-built agents and platforms (newer, growing fast)
5. **UX design** — historically their differentiation; portals, intranets, mobile experiences

The shift to **AI-first** is the company's current strategic bet. They're moving from pure services toward a productized services model — reusable AI agents + forward deployed engineering to customize them.

## 2. Corporate History & Ownership

### The Rollup Story

newRocket as it exists today is the result of a private equity rollup:

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2021 | **Gryphon Investors** (SF-based PE, $5B+ managed equity) acquires Highmetric, which then acquires newRocket |
| Jan 2022 | Highmetric acquires 4 companies in 4 months, then **unifies all 5 under the newRocket brand** |
| 2022 | Named to Inc. 5000 fastest-growing private companies |
| 2023 | ServiceNow Worldwide Customer Workflow + Creator Workflow Partner of the Year |
| 2024 | ServiceNow Employee Workflow Partner of the Year |
| Nov 2024 | **Harsha Kumar** named CEO |
| Apr 2025 | **Frank Palermo** named COO |
| Oct 2025 | Intelligent Agent Crew launched (9 AI agents) |

**Why this matters in an interview:** PE ownership means growth metrics, efficiency, and path-to-exit drive decisions. The leadership transition in late 2024–2025 signals a strategic pivot — Harsha and Frank were hired specifically to lead the AI-first transformation.

### PE Context: Gryphon Investors

Gryphon targets $50–300M investments in mid-market companies. Their playbook: acquire, consolidate, grow, exit. newRocket is in the "grow" phase, which means:
- Aggressive investment in AI products and talent
- Emphasis on reusable IP (agents, accelerators, platforms)
- Likely exit event (acquisition or IPO) in the medium term
- Metrics focus: revenue growth, margin expansion, client retention

## 4. Competitive Positioning

### The ServiceNow Partner Ecosystem

| Tier | Partners | ServiceNow Headcount | Approach |
|------|----------|---------------------|----------|
| **Global Elite** | Accenture, Deloitte, Infosys, Cognizant, KPMG, EY, HCLTech, Wipro | 5,000–23,000+ per firm | Platform-agnostic, broad but often shallow |
| **Elite (newRocket's tier)** | newRocket, and a handful of others | 500–1,000 | Specialized, deep platform expertise |
| **Pure-play peers** | Crossfuze, FlyForm, Unifii | Smaller | Similar focus, less scale |

### Why Enterprises Choose newRocket Over the Big 4

1. **Pure-play depth** — Every employee lives and breathes ServiceNow. No context-switching to Salesforce or SAP projects.
2. **Speed to value** — "Measurable outcomes in weeks, not months." Small, senior teams vs. large staffing pyramids.
3. **AI-first positioning** — Pre-built agent catalog and FlightPath.AI methodology. 44% of executives now prefer AI strategy specialists over legacy consultancies (HFS Research, Dec 2025).
4. **Direct senior access** — You work with the people who built the solution, not junior associates following a playbook.
5. **Award pedigree** — Multiple ServiceNow Partner of the Year awards validate delivery quality.
6. **UX heritage** — Originally the UX leader in the ServiceNow ecosystem (portals, intranets, mobile experiences).

### Why They Might Lose Deals

- Smaller scale (500–1K vs 18K+ at Accenture)
- Less global delivery reach
- Less name-brand recognition with CIO-level buyers who default to Big 4
- PE-backed with some employee turnover concerns

### Market Tailwinds

The macro environment strongly favors newRocket's positioning:
- **Now Assist ACV passed $600M** in late 2025, targeting $1B by end of 2026 — fastest-growing product in ServiceNow history
- **55% of executives open to switching implementation providers** (HFS Research)
- **2/3 of ServiceNow implementations stall after first wave** — creating demand for specialists who can push past pilots
- 2026 is "the year of agentic collaboration in the enterprise" per ServiceNow's Amit Zavery

## 6. What to Emphasize in Your Interview

Based on newRocket's positioning and strategy:

- **AI-first mindset** — Show you think about AI as a product, not a feature. They're building reusable IP, not one-off consulting deliverables.
- **Speed and pragmatism** — "Weeks, not months" is their mantra. Demonstrate you can ship fast.
- **Client empathy** — This is a forward deployed role. Show you understand that the customer's business problem is the real problem, not the technology.
- **ServiceNow curiosity** — You don't need deep ServiceNow experience, but showing you've done your homework on the platform signals serious interest.
- **Enterprise sensibility** — Security, governance, scalability, compliance. Enterprise clients care about these things deeply.
- **Product thinking** — They want patterns from client work to flow back into reusable products. Show you think about "one-off vs. generalizable."
