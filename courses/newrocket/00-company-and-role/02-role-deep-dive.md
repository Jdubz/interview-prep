# 02 – Role Deep Dive: Forward Deployed AI Engineer

What this role actually is, what your days will look like, and how to position yourself for it.

---

## 1. What "Forward Deployed" Means

### Origin Story

Palantir invented the Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) role in the early 2010s (originally called "Delta"). Until ~2016, Palantir had more FDEs than traditional software engineers. The model has since spread to OpenAI, Anthropic, Ramp, Salesforce, Commure, and now newRocket.

### FDE vs. Adjacent Roles

| Dimension | Software Engineer | Solutions Engineer | Consultant | Forward Deployed Engineer |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|------------|--------------------------|
| **Primary output** | Code & systems | Demos & technical sales | Advice & strategy | Deployed, working solutions |
| **Customer exposure** | None/minimal | Pre-sale | Advisory | Embedded with customer |
| **Code ownership** | Owns product code | Doesn't write production code | Doesn't code | Writes production code in customer environments |
| **Success metric** | Features shipped | Deals closed | Reports delivered | Customer outcomes achieved |
| **Feedback loop** | Product roadmap | Sales pipeline | Engagement scope | Product + customer outcomes |
| **Ambiguity level** | Defined requirements | Defined product | Defined engagement | Undefined problems |

**The key distinction:** FDEs write production code AND work directly with customers. You're not advising — you're building. You're not building in isolation — you're building in context.

Palantir's description: "FDE responsibilities look similar to those of a startup CTO: you'll work in small teams and own end-to-end execution."

### How It Maps to newRocket

At newRocket, the FDE role sits in the **Agentic AI department** and reports to the **Global AI Center of Excellence Lead**. You are the person who:

1. **Embeds with enterprise clients** during FlightPath.AI engagements (4-week sprints)
2. **Implements and customizes AI agents** (from the Intelligent Agent Crew) for specific customer environments
3. **Builds integrations** between ServiceNow, external APIs, and AI services
4. **Translates business problems** into technical architectures
5. **Feeds patterns back** to newRocket's product teams for platform improvements

## 2. Day-to-Day Reality

### Time Split (Approximate)

```
35%  Client-facing work (discovery, workshops, demos, check-ins)
40%  Hands-on building (scripting, integration, configuration, prototyping)
15%  Internal product contribution (reusable components, platform feedback)
10%  Learning and enablement (ServiceNow certs, AI research, internal knowledge sharing)
```

### A Typical Engagement Lifecycle

```
Week 1: DISCOVER
├── Join client kickoff (understand their ServiceNow environment)
├── Discovery sessions: pain points, current workflows, data landscape
├── Map business requirements to AI opportunities
├── Identify which Agent Pack agents apply (Phoebe for IT, Ariel for HR, etc.)
└── Propose technical architecture

Weeks 2-3: PROTOTYPE
├── Configure ServiceNow environment (scoped app, tables, ACLs)
├── Deploy and customize selected agents
├── Build integrations (REST APIs, Integration Hub spokes)
├── Connect to customer's data sources
├── Implement RAG pipelines if needed (knowledge bases, embeddings)
├── Daily/frequent check-ins with client stakeholders
└── Iterate based on feedback

Week 4: DEMONSTRATE
├── Showcase working prototype with real customer data
├── Present impact benchmarks and value metrics
├── Document architecture and handoff materials
├── Define roadmap to production scale
├── Identify reusable patterns for newRocket platform
└── Transition to next engagement or production support
```

### Travel

~25% travel. This likely means 1 week/month onsite with clients for kickoffs, critical workshops, or go-lives. Rest is remote.

## 4. First 6 Months Success Criteria

The job posting explicitly states what "success" looks like:

1. **Deploy AI solutions across multiple client engagements** — You'll be on 3-6 FlightPath.AI sprints
2. **Deliver working workflows within ServiceNow environments** — Production-ready, not just demos
3. **Build trusted relationships** with customers and internal teams — People want to work with you
4. **Contribute reusable components** to newRocket platforms — Your client work generalizes into product
5. **Provide actionable product feedback** — You identify gaps and patterns from the field

### What This Tells You

They expect you to be **immediately productive across multiple clients**. This is not a role where you sit in training for 3 months. The ramp is steep and learning happens on engagements.

## 5. Career Trajectory

### Within newRocket
- **FDE → Senior FDE → FDE Lead** — leading a squad of FDEs on larger engagements
- **FDE → AI Product** — moving into the product team building the Intelligent Agent Crew, platforms, and accelerators
- **FDE → AI CoE Leadership** — managing the Center of Excellence, defining methodology and standards

### Broader Market
The FDE skillset is one of the most in-demand in tech right now:
- a16z calls it "the hottest job in tech"
- FDE postings up 800–1000% in 2025
- Companies hiring FDEs: Palantir, OpenAI, Anthropic, Ramp, Salesforce
- The combination of engineering depth + client skills + AI knowledge is rare and highly compensated

## 6. Questions to Research Before Your Interview

Prepare thoughtful answers to and about these:

1. **"Why newRocket over a Big 4 consultancy or a pure AI company?"** — Pure-play ServiceNow depth + AI-first strategy + speed-to-value is a unique combination
2. **"Why forward deployed over a traditional SWE role?"** — You want to see impact directly, work on diverse problems, and combine technical and business skills
3. **"How would you ramp up on ServiceNow quickly?"** — newRocket University, certifications, learning-by-doing on engagements
4. **"What AI agents have you built or deployed?"** — Have concrete examples ready
5. **"How do you handle ambiguity with clients?"** — Stories of undefined problems you structured and solved
6. **"How do you decide if something should be a one-off or a reusable component?"** — Product judgment is key to this role
